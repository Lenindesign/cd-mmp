# Design System

## Overview

This promo is based on the Car and Driver marketplace calculator experience. The visual identity is editorial, practical, and high-contrast: a dark C/D blue hero surface, clean white calculator panels, concise labels, and cobalt action states. The motion should feel like a guided product tour, not a dealer ad: restrained, precise, and confident, with the calculator UI doing most of the selling.

## Colors

- **Hero Blue**: `#0A1628` - dark editorial canvas used behind the calculator.
- **C/D Cobalt**: `#1B5F8A` - primary CTA, active controls, and important numeric emphasis.
- **Cobalt Dark**: `#154D70` - deeper action accents and frame shadows.
- **Paper White**: `#FFFFFF` - calculator cards, content panels, and primary contrast.
- **Editorial Ink**: `#222222` - primary text on light surfaces.
- **Instrument Gray**: `#6B6B6B` - secondary text, helper copy, and metadata.
- **Rule Gray**: `#E5E5E5` - dividers and panel borders.
- **Verified Green**: `#26870D` - completed steps, budget success, and positive guidance.
- **Signal Red**: `#D2232A` - brand accent and warning emphasis only.

## Typography

- **Display / Headline**: Inter, system sans. Used for page titles, feature claims, and calculator numbers. Weights 700-800.
- **Body**: Inter, system sans. Used for explanatory text and product claims. Weights 400-600.
- **Labels**: Barlow Condensed, system sans fallback. Used for all-caps editorial labels, step names, and CTA text. Tracking is moderate, never decorative.
- **Editorial Serif**: Lora, Georgia fallback. Reserved for long editorial copy; not used in this short promo.

## Elevation

Depth comes from practical product frames: soft shadows behind screenshots, 1px borders, pale blue summary panels, and a subtle dot grid inspired by the calculator hero background. Avoid glossy reflections and heavy luxury-car shadows. The screen should feel like a product demo from an editorial brand.

## Components

- **Calculator Shell**: Large white rounded panel with step navigation, form controls, and an estimate sidebar.
- **Estimate Card**: Pale blue side panel with a large cobalt monthly payment and compact line-item table.
- **Wizard Stepper**: Circle-and-line progress control with blue current state and green completed state.
- **Review Breakdown**: Stacked cost rows with red/green semantic values and clear horizontal rules.
- **Marketplace Bridge**: Vehicle inventory and shopping-tool cards that connect the estimate to browsing next steps.
- **CTA Bar**: Solid cobalt button with uppercase label and compact radius.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

- Keep the calculator UI legible and central in every product beat.
- Use cobalt and green only when they signal action or completion.
- Show real numbers from the calculator: `$592/mo`, `$30,000`, `$38,491`.
- Use clean editorial labels and short utility copy.
- Let screenshots move with controlled pans, zooms, and reveals.

### Don'ts

- Do not use dealer-lot urgency, countdowns, or sales-badge clutter.
- Do not use purple/blue SaaS gradients or neon tech styling.
- Do not obscure the calculator screenshots with oversized copy.
- Do not make the video feel like a generic marketplace ad.
- Do not use unrelated vehicle imagery as a fallback for missing assets.
