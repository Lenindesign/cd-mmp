# Design System

## Overview

This walkthrough shows the Car and Driver auto loan calculator as an end-to-end product flow. The tone should feel like a confident editorial product demo: focused, practical, and low-pressure. The user interaction is the hero, supported by restrained cursor motion, click pulses, and short script cards.

## Colors

- Hero Blue: `#0A1628`
- C/D Cobalt: `#1B5F8A`
- Cobalt Dark: `#154D70`
- Paper White: `#FFFFFF`
- Editorial Ink: `#222222`
- Instrument Gray: `#6B6B6B`
- Rule Gray: `#E5E5E5`
- Verified Green: `#26870D`
- Signal Red: `#D2232A`

## Typography

- Display and body: Inter, Arial, sans-serif.
- Labels: Inter uppercase with generous letter spacing for video legibility.
- Keep all copy short enough to read in motion.

## Motion

- Cursor movement is direct and purposeful with click rings at meaningful decision points.
- Screen changes should crossfade with small position shifts, never hard jump.
- Use transform and opacity animation only for smooth exports.
- Hold each step long enough for the click target and outcome to read.

## Components

- Persistent browser frame containing the calculator screenshots.
- Left-side script card with the current step label and one clear instruction.
- Bottom progress rail showing where the shopper is in the five-step flow.
- Click ring and focus glow overlays for user actions.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do show real calculator screens from start to review.
- Do use precise click effects on cards, sliders, buttons, and review controls.
- Do keep padding consistent between script copy, browser frame, and progress rail.
- Do not use dealer urgency, cluttered promotional badges, or unrelated vehicle imagery.
- Do not obscure key calculator numbers with oversized overlays.
