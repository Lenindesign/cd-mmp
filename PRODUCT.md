# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Car shoppers and Car and Driver readers who want a trustworthy way to compare vehicles, understand rankings, evaluate incentives, and move from editorial research into shopping decisions. The primary audience is shoppers first, with C/D enthusiast credibility shaping the experience.

## Product Purpose

This marketplace helps users make confident car buying decisions by combining editorial authority, vehicle rankings, deal discovery, comparison tools, and practical ownership information. Success means the interface feels credible, clear, and useful without slipping into dealer-lot pressure or generic marketplace clutter.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, helpful, confident. The voice should feel like an expert editor sitting beside the shopper: direct, calm, informed, and willing to explain what matters without overselling.

## Anti-references

Avoid dealer-lot clutter, shouty sales graphics, countdown urgency, fake scarcity, stacked badges, and overloaded incentive panels. Avoid designs that feel like anonymous SaaS, soft app-store templates, or luxury-car cliches that trade C/D authority for gloss.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with editorial confidence: make the most important recommendation or comparison easy to understand at a glance.
2. Keep shopping pressure low: deals and CTAs should be clear and useful, not aggressive.
3. Make expertise legible: use rankings, specs, labels, and context to show why something matters.
4. Balance utility with magazine craft: marketplace surfaces should remain practical while carrying Car and Driver's editorial presence.
5. Respect decision fatigue: reduce redundant choices, keep filters understandable, and expose detail progressively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Respect reduced-motion preferences, maintain keyboard access and visible focus states, avoid color-only meaning, and keep contrast strong across editorial, deal, and account surfaces.
